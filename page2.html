The Relief and International Development Division's vision for the upcoming five years is inspired by the idea of establishing and cementing the role of the institution itself. As is well known, Qatar Red Crescent Society, as an emergency relief institution is an integral part of the humanitarian response network worldwide, bringing together 190 Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies worldwide. it is this distinguishing role and the principles represented help and by the Qatar Red Crescent Society which is its source of strength and the core motivation to reach those we seek to help and provide services and assistance to them in a way that alleviates their suffering and preserves their dignity through our planning and implementation of programs . The nature of Qatar Red Crescent Society's humanitarian interventions is in alignment with Qatar’s National Vision 2030 and the Sustainable Development Goals. Additionally, the National Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies, in accordance with their mandate, perform their role as auxiliary to their states during humanitarian relief contexts. In the same vein, the programmatic humanitarian interventions of the Qatar Red Crescent Society have been built the vision of cross-Sectoral complementarity. Qatar Red Crescent society has excelled the past years in the implementation of health programs through its knowledge and expertise in many countries in the Middle East, Africa and Asia. Additionally, it has sought to develop strong linkages between it and other sectors of intervention, particularly the Shelter, Water and Sanitation and Food Security sectors. This ensures that our interventions begin and end in a manner that benefits the communities the Qatar Red Crescent Society has been entrusted to serve. It is the firm belief of the Qatar Red Crescent Society that its humanitarian response in all its programs and projects must contribute to the promotion of a culture of peace and non- violence. It will continue to improve its work through training and capacity building to acquire the skills and means necessary to ensure access to assistance is timely and effective for refugees, displaced persons and all vulnerable communities within its reach.


The Qatar Red Crescent is present in 18 countries worldwide: Philines, Myanmar, lraq, Turkey, Jordan, Lebanon, Libya, Central African Republic, Bangladesh, Nepal, Palestine, Afghanistan, Niger, Sudan, Somalia, and Yemen.

The total number of beneficiaries resulting from its relief and development projects during 2017 totaled 7,245,090 the total expenditure for international projects was 774.05 ,154,159 Qatari Rial.


Qatar Red Crescent Society's missions cover six main sectors of intervention: Health, Shelter, Food Security, livelihoods, NFI and emergency response, Water Sanitation and Hygiene and Capacity Building /Training.
The seven largest operations, which comprise 80 of the total spending for 2017 were Syria, Gaza, lraq, Somalia, Lebanon (for Syria's Regional Response ), West Bank and Afghanistan. in terms of its donors, Qatar Red Crescent Society is supported through many partnerships and funding opportunities in the region, whether through securing grants through local institutions- Education Above All, Eid charity, Qatar Charity- or grants from regional and international entities such as the Islamic Development Bank, UNICEF, UNHCR, UNOCHA and the ICRC.
In the state of Qatar, a total of 15 agreements have been concluded over the past year to finance the implementation of a series of long-term relief and development projects in counties where Qatar Red Crescent Society is located. These include: Emergency Response Project for Al-raqqa Displaced Persons, Humanitarian Multi – sector program in Ethiopia (WASH, Shelter and Livelihoods), Grant for the Construction of two Service Complexes in Sisi and Abu Surouj in West Darfur state (Sudan), Multi- Sector Program in Somalia (Health, Nutrition, Water Sanitation and Hygiene and Livelihoods).
